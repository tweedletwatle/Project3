Mitsubishi Motors
Japanese Auto Manufacturer

David worked for about 6 years for Mitsubishi. At the time, the company was the fastest growing car company in the United States. He had positions related to Incentives, Sales Operations, and Finance. After many poor product launches, Mitsubishi fell on tough financial times. David decided to leave the company because of those troubles.

Yamaha Motor Corporation
Japanese Motorsports Manufacturer

David was responsible for parts pricing during his 6 years at Yamaha Motor Corporation. Yamaha produces many different products in the power sports industry. They manufacture Motorcycles, ATVs, side by sides, personal water craft (Waverunners), jet boats, outboard motors, and generators. David thought Yamaha was a good company to work for and enjoyed his time there. However, they moved a lot of their operations to Georgia. David resigned because he did not want to live in Kennesaw, Georgia.

The Boeing Company
Major Aerospace Manufacturer

David joined Boeing in the parts pricing department. Boeing manufacturers a range of aerospace products. They build commercial airplanes like the 787, military aircraft like the F-15, F-18, Apache, and Chinook helicoptors. While at Boeing David traveled many times to the headquarters in Seattle. David liked Boeing because the work was stress free and the commute was less than a mile and a half. However, the work itself was not very challenging which is the reason he left after about 4 years.

Hyundai Motor Corporation
Korean Auto Manufacturer

David was responsible for parts pricing during his first 4 years at Hyundai Motor Corporation. After that, he managed the warranty analysis department . Hyundai has come a long way during its almost 40 year history in the US. When the brand launched, it was known for cheap cars that were not very dependable. They Hyundai Excel cost less than $5,000 but was known for its poor quality. Currently, Hyundai is known for its broad product line and high quality. Combined with sister company Kia, Hyundai has 10% market share in the United States.
