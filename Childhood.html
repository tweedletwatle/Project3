Pat Nixon Elemetary
Named after the first Lady who grew up in Cerritos

Pat Nixon's nickname was the Thunderbolts when David was in Kindergarten. The school mascot name changed when he was in 5th grade to Wolverines. This was probably due to a cold war era film called Red Dawn where some high school students manage to fight a guerilla war against invading communists. Their high school nickname was the Wolverines in that movie. The current mascot is the Jaguars. The school now calls itself Nixon Academy probably to sound more prestigous.

Whitney High School
David Spent 7th and 8th grade years here even though it is called a high school.

David did not enjoy his time at Whitney. The academics were accelerated and he preferred to spend his time playing the sport of the season. When it was football season, he played street football until the sun went down. Winters were spent playing 2 on 2 basketball on the basket set up on his driveway.

St. John Bosco
All Boys Catholic High School in Bellflower

David Spent his high school years at Bosco playing Basketball and Running Track. He excelled in track where his school record of 6'7" in High Jump stood for almost 20 years. At the time David believed that emphasis on sports was an advantage of the school. However, he now thinks the priorities of the school are mixed up and more emphasis should be placed on academics. Currently the school is nationally ranked in many sports.
